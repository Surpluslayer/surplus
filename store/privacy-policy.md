# surplus Chrome Extension — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: June 28, 2026_

The surplus Chrome extension ("the extension") lets you view your surplus
relationship book alongside your browser and capture LinkedIn profiles into
your surplus account. This policy explains what data the extension handles.

## What the extension accesses

- **LinkedIn profile information.** When you are viewing a LinkedIn profile
  page (`linkedin.com/in/...`), the extension reads the publicly displayed
  name, headline, and profile URL of that profile so it can show you who you
  are viewing and, if you choose, capture them into surplus.
- **Your surplus session.** The extension loads your surplus book
  (`event.surpluslayer.com`) in a side panel using the session cookie already
  set in your browser when you signed in to surplus. The extension never sees
  or stores your password.

## What the extension sends, and when

- The extension only sends a LinkedIn profile to surplus **when you click
  "Capture to surplus."** At that point the profile name, headline, and URL
  are sent to your own surplus account (`event.surpluslayer.com`) to create a
  contact and draft a message.
- The extension does **not** continuously upload, track, or transmit your
  browsing. It does not send data to any third party other than your surplus
  account.

## What the extension stores

- The extension keeps the most recently viewed profile in memory only, to
  populate the side panel. It does not maintain a separate database of your
  activity. Captured contacts live in your surplus account, governed by the
  surplus privacy policy.

## Data sharing and sale

- We do **not** sell your data. We do **not** share it with advertisers or
  third parties. Captured data goes only to your surplus account.

## Permissions, and why

- **Host access to `linkedin.com`** — to read the profile you are viewing.
- **Host access to `event.surpluslayer.com`** — to display your book and to
  capture profiles into your account.
- **Side panel, tabs, scripting, storage** — to show the panel, detect which
  LinkedIn page is in front, and inject the profile reader.
